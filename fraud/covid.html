Spotlight on: Benefit payment scams

When claiming a benefit

CRA My Account passwords may have been compromised.

Scammers are stealing identities to redirect CERB payments.

Refer to: Protect your identity: Using passwords

Scams include:

  • directing you to an apply on a fake website, where they collect your information, or
  • directing you to make a fraudulent claim
  • using your account name and password to file a claim on your behalf

You would only apply for a benefit if you met the eligibility criteria. All COVID-19 benefits are found here: Canada’s COVID-19 Economic Response Plan

If your benefit payments come from CRA:

  • We will never contact you by text or email to ask you to apply for or repay a benefit payment.
  • The only online way to claim your benefit is to sign-in to:
    • CRA's My Account, or
    • My Service Canada Account and then toggle over to your CRA My Account profile

When a scammer contacts you

If someone says they are from the Government

Many frauds and scams attempt to mimic real federal government services to gain access to your personal and financial information.

Refer to:

  • Identifying a Service Canada or 1 800 O-Canada fraudulent call
  • Identifying a Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) fraudulent call

If someone says they are from a financial institution

The Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC) is warning Canadians to be very cautious when receiving emails or text messages that appear to be from a financial institution asking for personal or financial information.

Refer to: Financial institution fraudulent call

Reporting fraud

If you a scam has happened to you, you will need to:

  • Collect your thoughts
  • Contact your financial institutions
  • Contact the police
  • Report the incident
  • Protect yourself from future fraud

False and misleading claims

We have not approved any product to prevent, treat or cure COVID-19.

Claims to prevent, treat or cure COVID-19

Selling unauthorized health products or making false or misleading claims to prevent, treat or cure COVID-19 is illegal in Canada.

Refer to: Online complaint form for illegal marketing of drugs and devices

Choosing the right disinfectant

Health Canada has an frequently updated list of hard surface disinfectant and hand sanitizers that are likely to be effective for use against COVID-19.

Refer to: List of disinfectants with evidence for use against COVID-19

Combating disinformation

The Government of Canada is investing $3 million to help combat false and misleading COVID-19 information as well as the racism and stigmatization that are often the result.

Refer to: Supporting Canadians to think critically about online health information
