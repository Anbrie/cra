Types of COVID-19 scams

Benefit payment scams

Getting you to claim a benefit

This includes:

  • directing you to an apply on a fake website, where they collect your information, or
  • directing you to make a fraudulent claim

You would only apply for a benefit if you met the eligibility criteria. All COVID-19 benefits are found here: Canada’s COVID-19 Economic Response Plan

This includes:

Each benefit has its own repayment critera. All COVID-19 benefits are found here: Canada’s COVID-19 Economic Response Plan

We will never contact you by text or email to ask you to apply for or repay a benefit payment.

Job opportunity scams

Many scams target and take advantage of people looking for a job. This includes:

  • Counterfeit cheque
  • Financial agent
  • Car wrapping
  • Mystery shopper

Learn more: COVID-19 scams related to job opportunities

When a scammer contacts you

If someone says they are from the Government

Learn how to identify and handle a scammer making a:

  • Service Canada or 1 800 O-Canada fraudulent call
  • Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) fraudulent call

If someone says they are from a financial institution

Learn how to identify and handle a scammer making a:

  • Financial institution fraudulent call

Steps to take if you are a victim of fraud

If you a scam has happened to you, you will need to:

  • Collect your thoughts
  • Contact your financial institutions
  • Contact the police
  • Report the incident
  • Protect yourself from future fraud

For full details, refer to: What to do if you're a victim of fraud
