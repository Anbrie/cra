Recognizing a scam

If someone says they are from the Government

Many frauds and scams attempt to imitate government services in order to gain access to your personal and financial information. To identify legitimate communications from us, be aware of these guidelines and know what to expect if we contact you.

  • Identifying a Service Canada or 1 800 O-Canada fraudulent call
  • Identifying a Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) fraudulent call

If someone says they are from a financial institution

Be aware when receiving emails or text messages that appear to be from a financial institution asking for personal or financial information.

Reporting

If you or a family member have been contacted by a scammer, report it the Canadian anti-fraud center.

  • Report fraud to the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre
  • What to do if you are a victim of fraud
  • Report deceptive marketing practices of services or products
    Notify the Competition Bureau if you believe that a company or an individual has contravened any of the several Canadian ‘Acts’ that regulate product sales and packaging in Canada.
  • Reporting unauthorized health products and claims
    Selling unauthorized health products like drugs, medical devices and natural health products or making false or misleading claims to prevent, treat or cure sickness and disease is illegal in Canada. Find out how to report and see incidents that have been investigated.
