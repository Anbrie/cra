How COVID-19 benefits impact your taxes - Personal income tax

The COVID-19 benefits are:

  • taxable
  • added to your other income sources, so you need to file a return
List of COVID-19 benefits you may have received
  • Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)
  • Canada Emergency Student Benefit (CESB)
  • Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB)
  • Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB)
  • Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit (CRCB)

Getting the tax slips

You will receive a tax slip in the mail:

  • T4A (for benefits issued by the CRA)
  • T4E (for benefits issued by Service Canada)

You can view tax slips online as of February 2021 in My Account.

If you use Auto-fill my return to file online, the information from these tax slips will be automatically entered into your return, saving you time and effort.

Filing and owing

You may owe tax when filing your return. This will depend on your personal circumstances, and the type of COVID-19 benefits you received:

  • CERB or CESB:
    • no tax was withheld when payments were issued
    • you may owe this tax when filing your 2020 tax return
  • CRB, CRSB, or CRCB
    • 10% tax was withheld at source but this may not be all the tax you need to pay
    • you may need to pay more (or less), depending on how much income you earned in 2020

There may be impacts to filing your tax return that are specific to the COVID-19 benefit you received, or if you are a resident in Quebec.
