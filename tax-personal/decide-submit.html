Before doing your taxes

Decide how to submit your taxes - Personal income tax

To make the decision about the best option for you, you may also want to consult What’s new page to see if any service changes or current events may affect your situation.

You are doing your own taxes

Using automated solutions

If you do your own taxes, automated tools will do the calculations for you.

  • Option 1: Complete and send your tax return online through Netfile certified software

    Cost: Free or varies

  • Option 2: Answer questions over the phone through File my Return, if you have low or fixed incomes and your situation remains unchanged year-to-year.

    Cost: Free

Using a paper tax guide
  • If you do your own taxes using a paper tax return, you must do the calculations yourself. Get a paper tax return

    Cost: Free

Note: The Federal Income Tax and Benefit Guide also provides instructions for filing out the paper return.

Someone else is doing your taxes

  • Using a representative
    A representative is a person or business that you authorize to deal with the CRA for you. This person could be your spouse or common-law partner, other family member, friend, or accountant.

    Cost: Free or varies

  • Using a discounter
    A discounter is a tax preparer who calculates your refund, and immediately pays you part of your refund—called a discounted tax refund—before filing your income tax and benefit return with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

    Cost: Varies

  • Using a free tax clinic
    If you have a modest income and a simple tax situation, volunteers at a free tax clinic may be able to complete your tax return for you. Free tax clinics are generally offered between February and April across Canada, while some are open year-round.

    Cost: Free
