Decide how to submit your taxes - Personal income tax

You do your own taxes: Option 1 of 2

Using automated tools

  • Online: Complete and send your tax return online through Netfile certified software
    • Who can use: Anyone
    • Calculations are done for you: Yes
    • Cost: Free or varies
    • Processing time:
      • Generally: Within 2 weeks
      • Today: Check CRA processing times
  • By phone: Answer questions using an automated phone system to file your return using File my Return
    • Who can use: Low or fixed incomes and your situation remains unchanged year-to-year
    • Calculations are done for you: Yes
    • Cost: Free
    • Processing time:
      • Generally: Within 2 weeks
      • Today: Check CRA processing times

Using a paper tax guide

  • If you do your own taxes using a paper tax return, you must do the calculations yourself. Get a paper tax return
    • Who can use: Anyone
    • Calculations are done for you: No
    • Cost: Free
    • Processing time:
      • Generally: Within 8 weeks

Someone else does your taxes: Option 2 of 2

  • Using a representative

    A representative is a person or business that you authorize to deal with the CRA for you. This person could be your spouse or common-law partner, other family member, friend, or accountant.

    • Who can use: Anyone
    • Calculations are done for you: Yes
    • Cost: Free or varies
    • Processing time:
      • Generally: Within 2 weeks
      • Today: Check CRA processing times

  • Using a discounter

    A discounter is a tax preparer who calculates your refund, and immediately pays you part of your refund—called a discounted tax refund—before filing your income tax and benefit return with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

    • Who can use: Anyone
    • Calculations are done for you: Yes
    • Cost: Varies
    • Processing time:
      • Generally: Within 2 weeks
      • Today: Check CRA processing times

  • Using a free tax clinic

    Volunteers at a free tax clinic may be able to complete your tax return for you. Free tax clinics are generally offered between February and April across Canada, while some are open year-round.

    • Who can use: Those with a modest income and a simple tax situation
    • Calculations are done for you: Yes
    • Cost: Free
    • Processing time:
      • Generally: Within 2 weeks
      • Today: Check CRA processing times
