Tax numbers (SIN, ITN, or TTN) - Personal income tax

A tax number identifies you for income tax purposes and is needed to work in Canada, or to receive benefits and services from government programs. There are three different tax number types:

Social insurance number (SIN)
  • A nine (9) digit number where the first digit indicates the province of registration:
    • Starts with "1" = Issued in: NB, NF, NS, PE
    • Starts with "2" = Issued in: QC
    • Starts with "3" = Issued in: QC
    • Starts with "4" = Issued in: ON
    • Starts with "5" = Issued in: ON
    • Starts with "6" = Issued in: AB, MB, SK, NT, NU
    • Starts with "7" = Issued in: BC, YU
    • Starts with "8" = Not Used
    • Starts with "9" = Immigrants & other temp SIN's
    • Starts with "0" = Not Used
  • Issued to: Canadian citizens, permanent residents or a temporary residents
  • Issued by: Service Canada
  • Learn more: Social insurance number (SIN)
Individual tax number (ITN)
  • A nine (9) digit number beginning with "08" or “09”.
  • Issued to: Non-residents or international students who are unable to obtain a SIN but are required to file a tax return for ITA purposes
  • Issued by: Canada Revenue Agency
  • Learn more: Individual tax number (ITN)
Temporary tax number (TTN)
  • A nine (9) digit number beginning with a "01" or "03".
  • Issued to: A taxpayer who resides in Canada for ITA purposes but cannot obtain a SIN
  • Issued by: Canada Revenue Agency
  • Learn more: Temporary tax number (TTN)
