Payment deadline for your 2020 taxes

The payment and filing deadline for 2020 taxes is April 30, 2021.

It is important to file your tax return on time to ensure your benefit payments and other credits continue without disruption. If you owe taxes, it is equally important to pay your balance owing by the deadline to prevent additional interest and penalties from being added to the amounts you already owe.

If you file and pay any amounts you owe by the deadline, you will avoid:

  • a daily compound interest that starts on May 1, 2021 on unpaid amounts
  • a 5% late-filing penalty that applies if you have a balance owing, plus an additional 1% for each month (up to a maximum of 12 months) that you file after April 30, 2021

Pay your amount owing

When you file your return and have an amount on line 48500, this means you have a balance owing. If this amount is less than $2, you do not have to make a payment.

If you pay the full amount before the deadline, you do not have to pay interest or a late-filing penalty.

If you received a notice of assessment (NOA) showing a balance owing, you can pay the amount you owe in arrears.

You have several options to pay your taxes, including online, in-person, or using a third-party service provider. When making your payment, double-check that you are putting your payment towards the right debt you want to pay.

If you cannot pay in full

If this is your first time with a tax debt or if you are unsure of what do next, the CRA can work with you to find the best way to resolve your debt.

If you are not able to pay the full amount before the deadline:

  • you can avoid paying a late-filing penalty, if you file your return on time
  • you can make a partial payment to pay what you can afford which reduces the amount of interest you need pay on unpaid amounts

Use the following tools to help you determine what you can afford to pay on a regular basis:

  • Income and expense worksheet
  • Payment arrangement calculator

Options to pay

After you determined how much you can pay, there are several options you can choose from:

  • Use pre-authorized debit in My Account to set the payment amount you authorize the CRA to withdraw from your chequing account on the dates you choose
  • Contact the CRA to set up scheduled payments (payment arrangement) to be paid over time
  • Contact the CRA to pay an amount you can afford now and pay the rest of your tax debt as scheduled payments
  • Contact the CRA to discuss options if you cannot pay any of your balance owing

To contact the CRA about your payment options, you can call the CRA’s TeleArrangement service at 1-866-256-1147.

For more details: When you owe money - collections at the CRA

How to avoid owing taxes in the future

Ways to avoid future income tax debt:

  • Complete form TD1, Personal Tax Credits Return to allow your employer or payer to deduct additional tax from your pay cheque or your pension
  • Make voluntary payments to the CRA
