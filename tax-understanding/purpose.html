Purpose of taxes

Did you know that taxes pay for services that we all enjoy? A lot of things that we use daily, such as our schools, roads, and playgrounds are paid for by people like you.

What is tax

Tax is a mandatory payment made to a government. You pay taxes when you buy stuff and when taxes are deducted from your paycheck and paid to the government. Taxes are then used to pay for things that everyone uses.

Canada's tax system

The Canadian tax system is founded on self-assessment and voluntary compliance. This means that you are responsible for filing your taxes every year to see if you owe a debt or receive a refund.

The fairness and integrity of the self-assessment tax system is rooted in compliance with the tax rules – that is, the right people paying the right amount of tax at the right time.

What the Canada Revenue Agency does

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) is in charge of collecting taxes and other amounts, such as:

  • federal, provincial, and territorial income tax
  • goods and service tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST)
  • Canada Pension Plan contributions
  • employment insurance premiums

How the government spends taxes

In Canada, taxes are used by the government to pay for programs and services. Many every day things are made possible by taxes, such as:

  • education and schools
  • health care and hospitals
  • roads and bridges
  • police, ambulance, and fire services
  • libraries
  • parks and playgrounds
  • arenas
  • swimming pools
  • garbage and recycling collection
  • economic development
  • wildlife conservation
  • national defence

Taxes also fund social programs like:

  • Old Age Security
  • employment insurance
  • Canada child benefit
  • social assistance
