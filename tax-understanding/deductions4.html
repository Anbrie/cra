Other taxes you can expect to pay

Paul is at the mall with his friend Anna, buying a new shirt. When the cashier rings up the sale, the total is more than Paul expects.

"The price tag shows $24.95," protests Paul. The cashier checks the receipt and explains, "Yes, $24.95 plus tax."

Goods and services tax / Harmonized sales tax

The goods and services tax (GST) is a federal tax you pay on most goods and services you purchase in Canada. In some provinces, the GST has been combined with provincial sales tax to create the harmonized sales tax (HST). HST rates vary by province.

For in-depth information, refer to: Goods and services tax / Harmonized sales tax.

Provincial sales tax

Some provinces apply provincial sales tax or retail sales tax to the purchases you make. The items that are taxed and the tax rate vary from province to province.

For in-depth information, refer to: Provincial and territorial tax rates.

Excise taxes, duties, and levies

Excise duties and taxes, other levies and charges, and customs duties and tariffs collected by the federal government.

For in-depth information, refer to: Excise taxes, duties, and levies.

Property tax

Property taxes, usually charged by local governments on the value of land and buildings and paid by the property owner.
