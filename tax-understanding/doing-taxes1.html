Deductions off your paycheque

You must complete a tax return if you have to pay tax or if the Canada Revenue Agency sends you a request to file a return. Other reasons you must complete a tax return is if you are:

  • electing jointly to split pension income
  • disposing of capital property
  • repaying:
    • Old Age Security
    • Employment Insurance benefits
    • amounts you withdrew from your Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)
  • contributing to Canada Pension Plan (CPP) or Employment Insurance EI premiums on self-employment income or other eligible earnings
  • incurred a non-capital loss in the year that you want to be able to apply in other years.
  • carrying forward to a future year the unused investment tax credit on expenditures you incurred during the current year
  • filing a return for someone who has deceased as the legal representative (the executor, administrator, or liquidator)

Even if you don't owe taxes

Even if none of the above requirements apply, you may want to file a return if you are in one of the following situations:

  • claiming a refund
  • claiming a benefit, such as:
    • Canada child benefit
    • Goods and Services Tax / Harmonized Sales Tax (GST/HST) credit
    • Provincial or Territorial benefits
    • Working income tax benefit
  • carrying forward or transfer of unused tuition
  • keeping your saving plan or pension plan deduction limit up to date (registered retirement savings plan (RRSP), a pooled registered pension plan (PRPP), or a specified pension plan (SPP))

For in-depth information, refer to: Your tax obligations – Personal income tax
