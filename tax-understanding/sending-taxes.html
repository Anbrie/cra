After sending us your taxes

Getting a refund

When you complete your return online and sign up for direct deposit, you may get your refund in as little as eight days. Direct deposit is a fast, convenient, reliable, and secure way to get your payments deposited directly into your account at a financial institution in Canada.

For in-depth information, refer to: Refunds.

Owing a balance/debt

A balance owing is due April 30th. You will be charged compound daily interest starting May 1st, on any outstanding balance.

For in-depth information, refer to: Payments.

Changing your return

If you need to change your return after sending it to the Canada Revenue Agency, do not file another return for that year. Wait until you get your notice of assessment before you ask to change your return.

For in-depth information, refer to: Changing your tax submission.

Disagreeing with your assessment

If you disagree with your assessment, you should call the Canada Revenue Agency to discuss the matter. Many misunderstandings are resolved this way. If you are still not satisfied, you can file an objection which is the first step in the formal process of resolving a dispute.

For in-depth information, refer to: Disagreeing with CRA.

Protecting yourself against fraud

Be vigilant if you receive an email, text message, phone call or other communication that claims to be from the Canada Revenue Agency. A fraudulent communication could try to coerce you into paying a fictitious debt, or get you to enter personal information on a fake website. Know how to recognize a scam and learn how you can protect yourself against fraud.

For in-depth information, refer to: Protect yourself against fraud.

Keeping your records

Keep your supporting documents for six years. The Canada Revenue Agency may ask for supporting documents other than official receipts (such as cancelled cheques or bank statements), as proof of any deduction or credit you claim.

For in-depth information, refer to: Keeping your records.
