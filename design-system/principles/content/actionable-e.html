Make content actionable: CRA design system

Most people come to a government website to complete a task. They expect us to provide information that will help them. Tasks can be:

  • transactional (submit a form or download a report)
  • informational (find out how to collect a benefit or find a phone number)

People who want to complete a task online usually don't read word by word. They scan quickly for words and links related to what they want to do.

To help people complete tasks:

  • Determine user tasks
    then make the information tailored to their needs
  • Identify users' keywords
    this will help make the task intuitive and easy to understand
  • Get to the point
    be comprehensive in what needs to be done and take out everything that doesn't help complete the immediate task
  • Provide content at the point of need
    don’t send people searching elsewhere, and avoid FAQs
  • Ensure consistency and predictability
    use a standardized approaches and patterns so that people can scan content
