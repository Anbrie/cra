Make content accessible and inclusive: CRA design system

Accessibility barriers will create challenges for every person who uses your service. Think of accessibility barriers as usability barriers that you need to fix.

Being inclusive is about respecting diversity of lived experiences and perspectives across gender, sexuality, social class, age, culture and ability.

To make content accessible and inclusive:

  • Consider the needs of the audience
    ensure your service is usable by those who will find it most challenging and use words and images that respect differences
  • Determine the right format
    prioritize usability over engagement, as these two often have different objectives and produce the opposite results
  • Write in plain language
    Make it easy for all people to understand what they need to do
  • Check that content is easy to understand

  • Check the reading order using a keyboard

  • Give enough time to read and use content
    People who are stressed, have a motor or cognitive disability will often need more time to complete tasks before they time out
  • Make images perceivable to all
    People who can’t see need you to describe images for them in text
  • Make vidoes perceivable to all
    By providing captions and transcripts for your videos you make them accessible to people who can’t see and those who can’t watch them in certain environments - noisy spaces, slow internet
  • Use effective content structure
    Make content scannable and help people navigate using a screen reader
  • Write useful page titles and headings
    so that people can navigate content using these indicators as an outline
  • Use links effectively
    so that everyone, including people using screen readers, understands where links will take them
