Make content findable: CRA design system

There are many reasons why people may not be able to find what they are looking for:

  • Too much information
  • Not enough information
  • Not the right information
  • Not sure if it is the right information
  • Cannot understand the information

On average, people spend less than 15 seconds on a website before deciding if they found what they need.

Example: UX testing results for COVID-19 Travel landing page testing

Test date: July 9, 2020

Task
Farouk lives in Indonesia, and his brother is getting married in British Columbia in August. He plans on being in Canada for 20 days. Will Farouk be able to enter Canada?
Correct answer
No, he will not be able to enter Canada
Usability testing results
10/10 people gave the wrong answer (100% task failure)
Insight
Government definition of “immediate family” is too different from people’s mental model of what that means
Why this is important
Different mental models can prevent people from finding the answers they need

To make your content findable either when you create it or are improving it:

  • Have a content strategy
    so you don’t duplicate or produce contradictory information that will prevent users from finding the right answers
  • Start with the most important information
    people scan to find specific keywords, if they don’t see them, they may think this information does not exist
  • Create scannable content
    titles, headings, lists and whitespace help people find what they are looking for
  • Provide content at the point of need
    too much information will make it difficult for people to find answers
  • Ensure consistency and predictability
    use existing patterns and templates, they make it easier to understand and compare information
  • Use links effectively
    if people cannot understand the link text, they will not follow it
  • Make it easy to find content in search
    make sure your page title is clear in search results, people only look at a couple results on the top of the page to see if they found the right information

Result of poor findability

Of people who use Australian government services online:

50% have difficulty
finding information
24% of these users
call instead

Source: KPMG study: Digital Government Ease of Use Index 2015
