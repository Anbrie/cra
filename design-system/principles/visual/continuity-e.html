Law of continuity

On this page

  • Overview
  • Examples
  • Take aways

Overview

Definition: We perceive a line as an object that continues along the smoothest path. When objects cross over each other (for example lines) we perceive the two objects as two single lines, not four.

In practice: The eye has momentum as it moves from object to object. A grouping of content in a line, horizontally or vertically, increases this effect. Continuation is powerful, as it overrules many visual effects, including color. This is typically seen in graphic design, rather than web design.
