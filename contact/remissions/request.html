Pursue all other options before requesting remission

Remissions is a rare and extraordinary measure. As such, we won’t accept all requests for remission for review.

You must pursue all other options available to resolve your debt before requesting remission. In most cases, there are other ways we can help you address your debt.

Requesting a remission review does not stop collection actions

Based on what type of circumstances are you making a request?

  • Extreme financial hardship
  • Financial setback with an extenuating factor
  • Mistakes made by the CRA
  • Unintended results of the legislation
  • Other circumstances

What to include in your request

You or your authorized representative may request a remission review by sending a letter, with attachments, using 1 of the methods identified under Where to send your request.

We require a complete and verifiable account of your circumstances to conduct a remission review. If you don’t provide enough information, we won’t be able to accept your request.

All requests for a remission review must include the information in Identification and Details of request:

Identification

  • Taxpayer name
  • Account number
  • Authorized representative (if applicable)
  • Complete mailing address*
  • Daytime telephone number*

We require a mailing address and telephone number where we can contact you or your authorized representative about your remission request.

Details of request

  • Tax year(s) or period(s) the amount requested relates to
  • Dollar amount(s) of the debt
  • Type of debt (for example, tax, interest, penalty, or other debt)
  • Reason(s) you are requesting a remission review (see Situations that may be accepted for review)
Examples of documents or information you may need to provide

Depending on your request, here are some examples of what you should include:

  • Dates and descriptions of significant events related to your debt.
    • This might include how the debt arose, actions you took to try to resolve it, why you are now asking for a remission review.
    • We may not accept your request for review if you don’t tell us all your facts and circumstances.
  • Copies of any communications with or from us about the issue.
  • If you are requesting remission because you are in extreme financial hardship, include documentation of your family income since the time your debt arose.
  • If you believe there were extenuating factors in your case, describe how they impacted your ability to meet your tax, filing or payment obligations. Include any supporting documents that apply, for example medical records.
  • If you believe that we made an error or incorrectly informed you, include evidence of the error, for example, written instructions you received.
  • If you believe there have been unintended results of tax legislation in your case, explain, with proof, how the application of the legislation led to an inequitable outcome in your case.
  • Any other factors you believe are relevant to your remission request and any substantiation in support of your position
