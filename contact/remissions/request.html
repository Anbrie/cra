Pursue any other tax relief options before requesting remission

Remissions is a rare and extraordinary measure. Not all requests for remission are accepted by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for review.

Taxpayers are expected to pursue all other options available to resolve their debt before requesting remission. In the majority of cases, there are other ways the CRA can help taxpayers address their debts.

Requesting a remission review does not stop collection actions

You can continue to make payments toward your debt while we consider your request.

Call to discuss options to resolve your debt before making a request

If you would like to discuss options for paying your debt before requesting a remission review, you may call 1-800-959-8281 (individual enquiries) or 1-800-959-5525 (business and self-employed enquiries).

What to include in your request

Taxpayers or their authorized representatives may request a remission review by sending a letter, with attachments, to the CRA using one of the methods identified under Where to send your request.

The CRA requires a complete and verifiable account of your circumstances to conduct a remission review. If you provide insufficient information, the CRA will not be able to accept your request.

All requests for a remission review must include the information in Identification and Details of request:

Identification

  • Taxpayer name
  • Account number
  • Authorized representative (if applicable)
  • Complete mailing address*
  • Daytime telephone number*

The CRA requires a mailing address and telephone number where we can contact you or your authorized representative about your remission request.

Details of request

  • year(s) or period(s) the debt relates to
  • dollar amount(s) of the debt
  • type of debt (for example, tax, interest, penalty, or other debt)
  • reason(s) you are requesting a remission review (see eligible situations
Examples of how to document the reasons for your request

Depending on the reason for your remission request, here are some examples of the types of information you should include:

  • Dates and description of significant events related to your debt (for example, how the debt arose, actions you took to try to resolve it, why you are now asking for a remission review). If you do not fully disclose all material facts and circumstances, your request may not be accepted for review.
  • Copies of any communications with or from the CRA about the issue.
  • If you are requesting remission because you are in extreme financial hardship, include information concerning your family income since the time your debt arose.
  • If you believe there were extenuating factors in your case, describe how they impacted your ability to met your tax, filing or payment obligations. Include any supporting documents (such as medical records), if applicable.
  • If you believe that the CRA made an error or provided incorrect information, include evidence of the error or other substantiation (for example, written instructions from the CRA).
  • If you believe there have been unintended results of tax legislation in your case, explain (with supporting documentation) how the application of the legislation led to an inequitable outcome in your case.
  • Any other factors you believe are relevant to your remission request and any substation in support of your position
