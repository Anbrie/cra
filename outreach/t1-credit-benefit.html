You have access to different benefits and credits depending on your circumstances. If you file your tax return, you may get money back even if you had little to no income.

What you could be eligible for

Low to modest income earners

Individuals and families with low and modest incomes can get quarterly payments from the GST/HST credit. You may be eligible for other benefits, such as the Canada workers benefit and Canada child benefit.

Child, family and other benefits

Indigenous peoples

As an Indigenous person, you may be entitled to benefits, credits and deductions even if your income was tax exempt or you had no income.

Taxes and benefits for Indigenous peoples

New to Canada

If you left another country to settle in Canada, you may be entitled to benefits the year you arrive. New residents of Canada may be eligible to receive the GST/HST credit, the Canada child benefit, and provincial or territorial benefits and credits.

Taxes and benefits for newcomers

Person with disabilities or their supporting person

If you live with a disability or have a dependant with a disability, there are benefits, credits, and programs available to help you and the people who support you:

  • Disability tax credit
  • Child disability benefit
  • Canada caregiver credit
  • Home accessibility tax credit
  • Registered disability savings plan (RDSP)
  • Claim eligible medical expenses

Adults 65 years or older

Information on your tax return is used to determine eligibility for benefits and credits, as well as calculating payments like the Old Age Security pension. Even if you had no income, you must file your taxes to be eligible for:

  • GST/HST credit
  • Disability tax credit
  • Canada caregiver credit
  • Claim eligible medical expenses

More programs and services for seniors

Housing insecure, staying in a shelter or at risk of losing housing

When going through a difficult time, you need all the help you can get. There are many benefits, credits and programs available to help you:

  • GST/HST credit
  • Canada workers benefit (CWB)
  • Canada child benefit
  • Canada caregiver credit
  • Disability tax credit (DTC)
  • Child disability benefit

Students

As a student, you can claim tuition amounts on your tax return. Even if you don't earn any income, you can be eligible for benefits and credits if you file your taxes each year.

Get help with your taxes

You have several ways to do your taxes, including options that are free or are inexpensive. If you have a modest income and a simple tax situation, you may be able to get your taxes done by a volunteer – for free.
