What could you be eligible for

Low to modest income earners

Life is expensive and it can be challenging to keep up with the bills. Some benefits and credits that could help you if you earn a low income include the GST/HST credit and the Canada child benefit.

Child and family benefits

Indigenous peoples

As an Indigenous person, you may be entitled to benefits, credits and deductions even if your income was tax exempt or you had no income. Do your taxes each year and you may be eligible to receive the GST/HST credit and the Canada child benefit, and more.

Taxes and benefits for Indigenous peoples.

New to Canada

If you left another country to settle in Canada, you may be entitled to benefits the year you arrive. If you become a resident of Canada for income tax purposes, you may be eligible to receive the GST/HST credit and the Canada child benefit.

Taxes and benefits for newcomers.

Person with disabilities or their supporting person

If you live with a disability or have a dependant with a disability, there are benefits, credits, and programs available to help you and the people who support you:

  • Disability tax credit
  • Child disability benefit
  • Canada workers benefit
  • Canada caregiver credit
  • Home accessibility tax credit
  • Registered disability savings plan (RDSP)
  • Claim eligible medical expenses

Adults 65 years or older

Information on your tax return is used to determine eligibility for benefits and credits, as well as calculating payments like the Old Age Security pension. Even if you had no income, you must file your taxes to be eligible to get access to:

  • GST/HST credit
  • Disability tax credit
  • Canada caregiver credit
  • Claim eligible medical expenses

Programs and services for seniors

Housing insecure, staying in a shelter or at risk of losing housing

When going through a difficult time, you need all the help you can get. There are many benefits, credits and programs available to help you:

  • GST/HST credit
  • Canada workers benefit (CWB)
  • Canada child benefit
  • Canada caregiver credit
  • Disability tax credit (DTC)
  • Child disability benefit

Students

Once you start working, you will need to file your taxes each year to be eligible for benefits and credits:
