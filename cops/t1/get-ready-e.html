Steps to get ready for your 2020 taxes

  1. How COVID-19 benefits impact your taxes

    How to report your benefit amounts on your tax return, resolve tax slip issues, and what to do if you owe an amount.

  2. Choose how to submit your taxes

    File your own taxes, get your taxes completed by an authorized representative, tax preparer or free tax clinic.

  3. Get your documents ready

    • Tax slips

      Understand your tax slips, when you will receive them, and how to get a copy.

      • Ways to reduce your taxes owing

      • Find all your deductions, credits and expenses

        Find tax deductions, credits, and expenses to reduce the amount of tax you need to pay.

    • Other income or assets

      Gather documents for other income sources (including foreign investments) or assets that do not appear on tax slips.

  4. Key dates for the 2020 tax year

    Filing and payment due dates for taxes, contributions, instalment payments, and any amounts you may owe.

    Dates for filing your 2020 tax return

    • Feb 22, 2021: Earliest you can file online
    • Mar 1, 2021: Deadline to contribute to a RRSP, PRPP or SPP
    • Apr 30, 2021: Deadline to file your taxes
    • Jun 15, 2021: Deadline to file your taxes (if you’re self-employed or have a spouse or a common-law partner who is self-employed)

    Dates for paying your 2020 tax return

    • Apr 30, 2021: Deadline to pay your taxes (applies to everyone)

  5. Keep your CRA information up-to-date

    • Update your personal information

      Notify us about changes to your address or marital status.

    • Set up or change direct deposit

      Update or sign up for direct deposit through the CRA or your financial institution to get payments and refunds faster.

  6. What's new for 2020 taxes

    Changes to benefits, credits and expenses for individuals and families during the COVID-19 pandemic and 2020 tax year.

  7. What to expect if we contact you

    Know what information the CRA may ask when contacting you by phone, email or mail.

  8. Understand your rights as a taxpayer

    Know your rights, entitlements, and obligations as a Canadian taxpayer when dealing with the CRA.

  9. Now you can: Do your taxes

    How to complete and submit your taxes, report income, claim deductions and credits.
