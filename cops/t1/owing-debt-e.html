Interest relief due to COVID-19

Interest relief will be given to Canadians who had a total taxable income of $75,000 or less in 2020 and received at least one of these COVID-19-related income support benefits in 2020:

  • Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)
  • Canada Emergency Student Benefit (CESB)
  • Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB)
  • Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit (CRCB)
  • Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB)
  • Employment Insurance benefits
  • Provincial emergency benefits

If you meet the criteria and have filed your 2020 tax return, the CRA will automatically apply interest relief on your 2020 taxes owing. You will not have to pay interest on any amount owing from your 2020 taxes until April 30, 2022.

This does not apply to late-filing penalties, so make sure you file your tax return before the due date. If you file late, you will be charged a 5% late-filing penalty on top of what you already owe, plus an additional 1% for each month (up to a maximum of 12 months).

Interest will start after April 30, 2022 on any amount owing for the 2020 tax year.

COVID-19 benefit payments

If you received emergency or recovery benefits, keep in mind these benefits are taxable. This means you will receive a T4A tax slip for the amount of benefits you received, and may owe taxes this year.

If you have repaid any benefits you were not entitled to before December 31, 2020, the CRA won't include that amount in the T4A tax slip.

How to avoid owing taxes in the future

Ways to avoid tax debt:

  • Complete form TD1, Personal Tax Credits Return to allow your employer or payer to deduct additional tax from your pay cheque or your pension
  • Make voluntary payments to the CRA
