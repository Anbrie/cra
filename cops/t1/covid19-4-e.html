Payment deadline for your 2020 taxes

The payment and filing deadline for 2020 taxes is April 30, 2021.

It is important to file your tax return on time to ensure your benefit payments and other credits continue without disruption. If you owe taxes, it is equally important to pay your balance owing by the deadline to prevent additional interest and penalties from being added to the amounts you already owe.

If you file and pay any amounts you owe by the deadline, you will avoid:

  • a daily compound interest that starts on May 1, 2021 on unpaid amounts
  • a 5% late-filing penalty that applies if you have a balance owing, plus an additional 1% for each month (up to a maximum of 12 months) that you file after April 30, 2021

Pay your amount owing

When you file your return and have an amount on line 48500, this means you have a balance owing. If this your first time with a tax debt or if you are unsure of what do next, the CRA can work with you to find the best way to resolve your debt.

You have several options to pay your taxes, including online, in-person, or using a third-party service provider. If you owe less than $2 in taxes, you do not have to make a payment.

If you are able to pay the full amount you owe by the deadline, you avoid paying interest and penalties on top of what you already owe.

Even if you cannot pay your balance owing on or before April 30, 2021, you can avoid the late-filing penalty, if you file your return on time.

If you are not able to pay the full amount all at once, you can make a partial payment to pay what you can afford. This reduces the amount of interest you need pay on unpaid amounts.

If you cannot pay in full

If you are not able to pay the full amount you owe to the CRA, we will work with you to resolve your tax debt.

How to avoid owing income tax in the future

There are many scenarios which may impact the amount of income tax you have to pay. For example:

  • you are self-employed
  • you have multiple pension or income sources
  • you took maternity or parental leave and received both a top up and EI benefits

Ways to avoid income tax debt:

  • Complete form TD1, Personal Tax Credits Return to allow your employer or payer to deduct additional tax from your pay cheque or your pension
  • Make voluntary payments to the CRA (instalment payments)
