Get ready to do your taxes - Personal income tax

Find out how COVID-19 benefits impact your taxes, what’s new for 2020, filing and payment due dates, and what options you have to file your tax return.

If you aren't sure if you should file, go to who should file a tax return.

Steps to get ready for 2020 taxes

  1. Find our what is new

    • How COVID-19 benefits impact your taxes

      Reporting COVID-19 amounts on your tax return, tax slip issues, options if you owe, and eligibility for interest relief.

    • What is new for 2020 taxes

      Changes to taxes, benefits, credits, and expenses for Canadians during the COVID-19 pandemic and the 2020 tax year.

  2. Key filing and payment deadlines for 2020 taxes

    Filing and payment due dates for taxes, contributions, instalment payments, and any amounts you may owe.

    Filing dates for 2020 taxes

    • Mar 1, 2021: Deadline to contribute to a RRSP, PRPP or SPP
    • Apr 30, 2021: Deadline to file your taxes
    • Jun 15, 2021: Deadline to file your taxes if you or your spouse or common-law partner are self-employed

    Payment date for 2020 taxes

    • Apr 30, 2021: Deadline to pay your taxes

  3. Report your income

    What to report as income, including COVID-19 benefits, self-employment income, foreign investments, assets, or other earnings.

  4. Get your documents ready

    Gather tax slips, receipts, and other supporting documents.

    • Tax slips

      Understand your tax slips like T4 and T4A, when you will receive them, and how to get copies.

    • Deductions, credits, and expenses

      Find tax deductions, credits, and expenses such as home office expenses for employees to reduce your tax owing or to get a refund.

    • Get a tax return

      Download an income tax package or order a paper return by mail.

  5. Keep your CRA information up-to-date

    • Update your personal information

      Tell us about changes to your address or marital status.

    • Set up or change direct deposit

      Update or sign up for direct deposit through the CRA or your financial institution to get payments and refunds faster.

  6. Choose an option to file your taxes

    File taxes yourself, either online or by paper, or find other options to have someone else complete your tax return for you.

  7. Find out if you have a refund or a balance owing

    • Getting a refund

      You will get a refund if you paid more taxes than needed.

    • Have a balance owing

      Pay taxes you owed, options if you cannot pay, or arrange to pay over time.

  8. Send us your completed tax return

    Use certified tax software or an authorized service provider to file your tax return electronically or file by mail.

  9. Review: After you file your tax return

    Pay a balance owing, get your notice of assessment, get the status of your refund, or make a change to your tax return.
