Who should file a tax return - Personal income tax

Find out if you should file a tax return and your tax obligations based on your situation.

Services and information

Canadian residents

You live and work in Canada, you have to pay taxes, and want to receive credits and benefit payments.

Indigenous peoples

Your benefits and credits, tax exempt income under the Indian Act, COVID-related benefits, and filing a tax return.

Non-residents of Canada

You do not have significant residential ties in Canada and are staying for less than 183 days in a year in Canada.

Newcomers to Canada (immigrants)

You left another country to settle in Canada and became a protected person (refugee) or permanent resident.

Deemed residents

You live in Canada for 183 days or more and do not have residential ties, and are not a resident of another country.

Live in Canada temporarily

You are a resident of another country, but live in Canada temporarily for work or for school.

Leave Canada permanently (emigrants)

You leave Canada to live in another country and no longer have residential ties with Canada.

Work, study, or travel abroad temporarily

You live outside of Canada temporarily for work, school, vacation, or health reasons.

File taxes for someone who died

Complete a final return, become a legal representative, request a clearance certificate, handle benefits and credits.
