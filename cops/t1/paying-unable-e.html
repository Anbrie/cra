Unable to pay - Personal income tax

If you cannot meet your debt obligations with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA), there are a few options you can take depending on your situation.

On this page

  • Contact us to discuss your situation
  • Consequences of not paying
  • Options available to assist debt repayment

Contact us to discuss your situation

You should always contact the CRA when you need help to meet your debt obligations.

Consequences of not paying

If you ignore your resposibilities or refuse to co-operate to eliminate your debt, the CRA may take legal action which could result in serious financial or legal consequences.

Refer to: Personal income tax debt collection - Consequences of not paying

Options available to assist debt repayment

Options available to assist debt repayment
Situation Summary More information
Debt deferral If you inability to pay is temporary, contact us to discuss deferring repayment until you are financially able to start a payment arrangment.
Taxpayer relief In some circumstances, you may ask for relief from penalties and interest, and reduce the amount you or your business owes. Taxpayer relief provisions
Insolvency or bankruptcy If you feel you are insolvent or are considering bankruptcy Office of the superintendent of bankruptcy
