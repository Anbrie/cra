Arrange to pay over time - Personal income tax

When you arrange to pay any existing debt over time, you are entering into a payment arrangement. A payment arrangement is an agreement with the CRA that allows you to make smaller payments over time.

On this page

  • Make a partial payment
  • Arrange to pay over time
  • Adjust your payment arrangement

Make a partial payment

Making a partial payment to the CRA still requires you to enter into a payment arrangement.

Arrange to pay over time

Before you make a payment arrangement, you may need to show that you have tried to pay your debt in full by borrowing money or reducing your expenses. To figure out your ability to pay, we may ask you to provide proof of your income, expenses, assets, and liabilities. You may have to do this by telephone or by completing a financial questionnaire.

  1. Determine how much you can pay

    Before you determine the amount and the frequency to be paid, the Personal income and expense worksheet is a helpful budget tool to help you determine what you can afford to pay on a regular basis.

  2. Propose a payment amount

    Use the Payment Arrangement Calculator to calculate the payment amounts based on amount you want to pay, or the frequency on when you want to pay down your debt. The calculator also includes the prescribed CRA interest rates.

  3. Set up your payment arrangement

    There are three ways you can set up a payment arrangement.

    Online

    Set up a payment arrangement using Pay by pre-authorized debit through your CRA My Account.

    In most cases, you can make the arrangement on your own without speaking with the CRA.

  4. Start to make your payments

    In order to keep your payment arrangement, you will need to make a payment on the agreed upon dates. There are many ways to make a payment.

    You must pay as agreed, continue to file all returns on time, and stay up to date with your tax obligations. Payment arrangements may be subject to periodic reviews.

    Even if you have a payment arrangement and are making payments, the CRA is authorized to take amounts from any benefits or credits you receive when you have a debt.

Adjust your payment arrangement

Should something happen and you need to adjust your payment arrangement or you are unable to pay, you must contact the CRA.

Failure to do this may cause the CRA to proceed with legal actions to collect the balance you owe.
