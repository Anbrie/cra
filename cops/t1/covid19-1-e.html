T4A slips for 2020 tax year

If you received COVID-19 emergency or recovery benefits from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA), you should get a T4A slip for the amounts you received.

These benefit amounts are taxable income. Any payments you received before December 31, 2020 needs to be reported on your 2020 income tax and benefit return.

You will get a T4A slip if you received:

  • Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) with the CRA
  • Canada Emergency Student Benefit (CESB)
  • Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB)
  • Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit (CRCB)
  • Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB)
  • Provincial or territorial COVID-19 financial assistance payments

You will receive one T4A slip which includes all your COVID-19 benefit payments from the CRA and any provincial or territorial government payments.

The CRA will send your T4A slip starting in January 2021 and you should receive it before March 10, 2021. You may also get a copy of your T4A slip from CRA My Account.

After March 10, 2021, if you still do not have your T4A information, contact the CRA.

Other tax slips you may receive

If you received CERB from Service Canada, you may also receive a T4E slip.

Quebec residents will get both a T4A slip and a RL-1 slip.

If the CRA makes an adjustment to amounts on your T4A slip, you will receive an amended T4A slip. Use the amended T4A slip to report your benefit payment amounts on your tax return.

If the CRA verifies that you received a T4A slip for COVID-19 benefit payments in error, you will receive a cancelled T4A slip. You do not need to report the cancelled T4A amounts on your tax return.

If your employer received the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS) to cover part of your wages, the income you earned will appear on your regular T4 slip.

Verify COVID-19 amounts on your T4A slip

All COVID-19 benefits you received from the CRA and any provincial or territorial payments received in 2020 will be shown on one T4A slip.

Each benefit will have its own box number (Box 197 to 204) on your T4A slip. If you received more than one benefit with the CRA or made repayments, verify your benefit amounts on your T4A slip are correct before you report them on your tax return.

To help verify your amounts, review your COVID-19 benefit payment amounts by selecting "COVID-19 Support Payment Application Details" in your CRA My Account.

If you repaid a benefit or paid a tax instalment

If you repaid a CRA COVID-19 benefit in 2020 or paid a T1 tax instalment, your payment may have been deposited to another of your CRA accounts. Be sure to review all notices from the CRA, including payment summaries, your T4A, and T4E, to confirm that the amounts are correct.

If any amounts are incorrect, they can be easily corrected by calling the CRA. Follow the instructions under Issues with you T4A slip.

If you repaid a COVID-19 benefit payment in 2021, the repaid amounts will appear on your next year's T4A slip. The repayments will have be applied to your 2021 taxes.

Issues with your T4A slip

Call the CRA right away

if you have any of the following issues:

  • your T4A slip shows an incorrect amount
  • you repaid benefit amounts before December 31, 2020, but the repayments are not reflected on your T4A slip
  • your T4A slip shows incorrect personal information
  • you did not apply for COVID-19 benefits, but received a T4A slip

Calling about a benefit repayment, have the following ready:

  • the benefit type (CERB, CESB, CRB, CRCB, CRSB)
  • the eligibility period repaid
  • the amount and date of your repayments

Repayments and impact on your taxes

You may have applied for CERB with the CRA, CESB, or recovery benefits (CRB, CRCB, CRSB), but later found you were not eligible.

If you repaid 2020 benefit amounts:

  • before December 31, 2020 - repaid amounts should be reflected on your 2020 T4A slip and you will not have to pay tax on those repaid amounts on your 2020 taxes
  • after December 31, 2020 – you will pay tax on the full amount shown on your 2020 T4A slip (repaid amounts will be adjusted on your 2021 taxes)

Report T4A amounts on your tax return

If you received a T4A slip, your COVID-19 benefit amounts are shown in boxes 197 to 204:

Find your T4A COVID-19 benefit box numbers

Add up each COVID-19 benefit payment amount you received from your T4A slip and enter the total amount on line 13000 of your tax return. If you applied for the CRB with more than $38,000 in net income and you are filing by paper, go to Complete the worksheet for the return for paper filing.

In the space to the left of line 13000, specify the type of income you are reporting. If you are filing a paper return and received more than one benefit, attach a list of all benefits received.

Report income tax deducted at source

If you received the CRB, CRCB or CRSB, the CRA withheld a 10% tax at source on each payment. Enter the income tax deducted from box 022 on line 43700 on your 2020 tax return.

Quebec residents will get both a T4A and RL-1 slip. The 10% tax withheld at source will be split between the 2 tax slips, 5% will be reported on the T4A slip and the other 5% will be reported on the RL-1 slip.

CRB repayments for net income above $38,000

If you received the CRB and your net income is more than $38,000 (excluding CRB amounts), you may have to repay part or all of the benefit you received.

Any repayments owed will be included as part of your total balance owing for your 2020 taxes.

Complete the worksheet for the return for a paper filing

If line 23400 is more than $38,000, use the chart for line 23500 on the worksheet for the return to calculate your social benefits repayment amount. This is the amount you need to repay.

You will have to repay $0.50 of the CRB for every dollar of net income you earned above $38,000. You will not have pay back more than what you received in CRB benefits for that year.

Steps to calculate the social benefits repayment on the worksheet
  1. Enter applicable amounts on lines 1 to 24
  2. Insert your CRB amount on line 25 (from box 202 on your T4A slip)
  3. Subtract your CRB amount from line 24 and enter the difference on line 26
  4. Subtract $38,000 (the base amount) from line 26
  5. Take the subtracted amount and multiply by 50%
  6. Use the CRB amount or the 50% calculation (whichever is less) to calculate the amount for line 23500
  7. Enter this amount on both line 23500 and line 42200 of your tax return

Enter the calculated amount from the worksheet on line 23500 and line 42200 of your tax return. You do not have to attach the worksheet to your return, but keep it for your records.

Filing your return electronically

If you are filing electronically with certified tax software, the amounts you need to repay should be calculated for you and added to your balance owing.

If your income is tax exempt

If your income is eligible for tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act, for details go to taxes and benefits for Indigenous peoples.
