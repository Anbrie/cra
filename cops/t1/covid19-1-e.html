Why you may receive tax slips

If you received COVID-19 emergency or recovery benefits before December 31, 2020, you should get a tax slip with the amounts you received. These benefit amounts are taxable and need to be reported as income on your 2020 income and benefit return.

You will get a tax slip if you received:

  • Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) with the CRA
  • Canada Emergency Student Benefit (CESB)
  • Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB)
  • Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit (CRCB)
  • Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB)
  • Provincial or territorial COVID-19 financial assistance payments

Types of tax slips you may receive

You will get a T4A slip, a T4E slip, or both, with the COVID-19 benefit amounts you received. This depends if you received your benefit payments from the Canada Revenue Agency or Service Canada.

If your employer received the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS) to cover part of your wages, the income you earned will appear on your regular T4 slip.

Benefits from CRA will get a T4A slip

If you received COVID-19 benefit payments in 2020 from the CRA or your provincial or territorial government, you will get a T4A slip starting in January 2021. You should receive your T4A slip before March 10, 2021.

Quebec residents will also get a RL-1 slip.

You may also get your T4A slip from CRA My Account.

After March 10, 2021, if you still do not have your T4A slip information, contact the CRA.

Use the amounts from your T4A slip to report your COVID-19 benefits on your tax return.

Verify amounts on your tax slips

When you receive your tax slip, verify the COVID-19 amounts you received and any repayments you made before December 31, 2020 are accurate.

Issues with your T4A slip

You will receive one T4A slip that includes all COVID-19 benefits you received from the CRA in 2020. Each benefit will have its own box number (Box 197 to 204) on your T4A slip. If you received more than one benefit with the CRA, check each benefit amount to make sure they are correct.

To confirm the COVID-19 benefit amounts you received, you can review "COVID-19 Support Payment Application Details" in CRA My Account.

Call the CRA right away if:

  • Your T4A slip shows incorrect amounts
  • You repaid benefit amounts before December 31, 2020, but the repayments are not reflected
  • Your T4A slip shows incorrect personal information
  • You did not apply for a COVID-19 benefit, but received a T4A slip

Report T4A amounts on your tax return

If you received a T4A slip, your COVID-19 benefit amounts will be shown in box numbers 197 to 204:

Find your T4A COVID-19 benefit box numbers

Add up each COVID-19 benefit amount you received from your T4A slip and enter the total amount on line 13000 of your 2020 income tax and benefit return.

In the space to the left of line 13000, specify the type of income you are reporting. If you are filing by paper and received more than one benefit, attach a list of all benefits in a note.

Report income tax deducted from recovery benefits

If you received the CRB, CRCB or CRSB, the CRA withheld a 10% tax at source on each payment. In addition to reporting the amounts on line 13000, enter the income tax deducted from box 022 on line 43700 on your 2020 tax return.

If your income is tax exempt

If your income is eligible for tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act, go to taxes and benefits for Indigenous peoples for more Information.

If you repaid CERB amounts

If you repaid CERB amounts after December 31, 2020, you need to pay tax on the full amount shown on your T4A slip. The CRA will adjust the repaid amounts on your 2021 tax return.

Find out if you need to repay CRB

If you received the CRB and your net income is more than $38,000 (excluding CRB amounts), you may have to repay part or all of the benefit.

If you are filing by paper and your amount on line 23400 is more than $38,000, use the worksheet for the return to calculate the social benefits repayment amount. This tells you how much you need to repay. You will have to reimburse $0.50 of the CRB for every dollar of net income you earned above $38,000.

Steps to calculate the social benefits repayment on the worksheet
  1. Enter applicable amounts on lines 1 to 24
  2. Insert your CRB amount on line 25 (from box 202 on your T4A slip)
  3. Subtract your CRB amount from line 24 and enter the difference on line 26
  4. Subtract $38,000 (the base amount) from line 26
  5. Take the subtracted amount and multiply by 50%
  6. Use the CRB amount or the 50% calculation (whichever is less) to calculate the amount for line 23500
  7. Enter this amount on both line 23500 and line 42200 of your tax return

You must enter the calculated amount from the worksheet on line 23500 and line 42200 of your tax return. You do not have to attach the completed worksheet to your return, but keep it for your records.

If you are filing electronically with certified tax software, the amounts you need to repay should be calculated for you and added to your balance owing.

If you have to repay part or all of your CRB, the repayments will be added to your total balance owing for your 2020 taxes. The same interest and penalties apply as any amounts you owe in taxes.
