Interest relief due to COVID-19 - COVID-19 benefits and your taxes

Interest relief will be given to people with a total taxable income of $75,000 or less in 2020 and received at least one COVID-19 benefit in 2020:

  • Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)
  • Canada Emergency Student Benefit (CESB)
  • Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB)
  • Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit (CRCB)
  • Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB)
  • Employment Insurance benefits
  • Provincial emergency benefits

If you meet the above criteria and have filed your 2020 tax return, the CRA will automatically apply interest relief on your 2020 taxes owing. You will not have to pay interest on any amount owing from your 2020 taxes until April 30, 2022.

File your taxes on time

Late filing penalties still apply. File your tax return before the due date. If you file late, you will be charged a 5% late-filing penalty on top of what you already owe, plus an additional 1% for each month (up to a maximum of 12 months).

Interest will start after April 30, 2022 on any amount owing for the 2020 tax year.
