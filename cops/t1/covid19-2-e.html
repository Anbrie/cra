Which tax slip will you receive

Where you enter your benefit amounts on your tax return depends if you received your benefit payments from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) or Service Canada:

  • T4A slip: benefits received from the CRA
  • T4E slip: Employment Insurance (EI) or CERB payments from Service Canada

If you find an error or have issues with your tax slips, go to Tax slips you receive for COVID-19 benefits.

You received a T4A slip

If you received a T4A slip, your COVID-19 benefit amounts will be shown in box numbers 197 to 204:

COVID-19 benefits - Box 197 to 204

If you are not eligible for the CERB and repaid amounts after December 31, 2020, the amounts will still show on your T4A slip. You need to report the full amounts on your 2020 tax return and may need to pay tax on those amounts. The repaid amounts will be adjusted on your 2021 tax return.

Find out what you need to do, if you have an issue with your T4A slip.

Report T4A amounts

Add up each COVID-19 benefit amount you received from your T4A slip and enter the total amount on line 13000 of your 2020 income tax and benefit return.

In the space to the left of line 13000, specify the type of income you are reporting. If you are filing by paper and received more than one benefit, attach a list of all benefits in a note.

Report income tax deducted from recovery benefits

If you received the CRB, CRCB or CRSB, the CRA withheld a 10% tax at source on each payment. In addition to reporting the amounts on line 13000, enter the income tax deducted from box 022 on line 43700 on your 2020 tax return.

If your income is tax exempt

If your income is eligible for tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act, the exempt income amount will be shown in box 144 of your T4A slip and does not need to be reported. For more details, go to: [Indigenous taxes]

If you repaid CERB amounts

If you repaid CERB amounts after December 31, 2020, you need to pay tax on the full amount shown on your T4A slip. The CRA will adjust the repaid amounts on your 2021 tax return.

Find out if you need to repay CRB

If you received the CRB and your net income is more than $38,000 (excluding CRB amounts), you may have to repay all or part of the benefit.

If you are filing by paper and your amount on line 23400 is more than $38,000, use the worksheet for the return to calculate the social benefits repayment amount. This tells you how much you need to repay. You will have to reimburse $0.50 of the CRB for every dollar of net income you earned above $38,000.

Steps to calculate the social benefits repayment on the worksheet
  1. Enter applicable amounts on lines 1 to 24
  2. Insert your CRB amount on line 25 (from box 202 on your T4A slip)
  3. Subtract your CRB amount from line 24 and enter the difference on line 26
  4. Subtract $38,000 (the base amount) from line 26
  5. Take the subtracted amount and multiply by 50%
  6. Use the CRB amount or the 50% calculation (whichever is less) to calculate the amount for line 23500
  7. Enter this amount on both line 23500 and line 42200 of your tax return

If you have to repay all or part of your CRB, the repayment amounts will be added to your total balance owing for your 2020 taxes. The same interest and penalties apply as any other amount you owe in taxes. The payment deadline is April 30, 2021 for the 2020 tax year.

You do not have to attach the completed worksheet to your return, but keep it for your records.

If you received a T4E slip

If you received your CERB benefit payments from Service Canada, your benefit amount will be included in box 14 of your T4E slip.

Your CERB benefit amount is not the only amount in box 14. It also includes regular employment insurance benefits and other benefits paid.

Steps for reporting T4E amounts on your tax return
  1. Find your total benefits paid amount in box 14 of your T4E slip
  2. If you are not eligible to receive tax exempt benefits under the Indian Act, take the amount in box 14 and subtract any amount from box 18 (under "Other information" on your T4E slip)
  3. Enter the subtracted amount on line 11900 - Employment insurance and other benefits on your return
  4. The amount from line 11900 is included as part the calculation of your total income

Find out if you need to repay any benefit amounts

To find out if you have to repay some of the benefits you received, check if your amount on line 11900 plus your net income (line 23400) is more than $67,750.

If the total is more than $67,750, fill in the repayment chart on the back of your T4E Statement of Employment Insurance and other benefits slip.

Enter the final amount from the repayment chart on lines line 23500 and line 42200 of your return.
