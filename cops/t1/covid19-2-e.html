T4E slips for 2020 tax year

If you received Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) with Service Canada or any Employment Insurance (EI) benefit payments, you should get a T4E tax slip with the amounts you received.

These benefit amounts are taxable income. Any payments you received before December 31, 2020 will need to be reported on your 2020 tax return.

You will receive a T4E slip before mid-March 2021 or you may also get your T4E information from My Service Canada Account.

Verify CERB amounts on your T4E slip

When you receive your T4E slip, make sure the CERB amounts you received are correct. You also need to verify any repayments you made before December 31, 2020 are accounted for.

CERB benefit payment amounts received from Service Canada are included in box 14 of your T4E slip. Your CERB benefit amount is not the only amount in box 14.

You can review your CERB benefit payment amounts in an itemized statement of your benefits from My Service Canada Account. You can verify the amounts you received matches the dates you received them.

Report an issue with your T4E slip

If you still do not have your T4E information or you have an issue on your T4E slip, contact Service Canada.

Report T4E amounts on your tax return

CERB benefit payment amounts received from Service Canada are included in box 14 of your T4E slip.

The total amount in box 14 includes your CERB amounts along with other employment insurance benefits.

Steps for reporting T4E amounts on your tax return
  1. Find your total benefits paid amount in box 14 of your T4E slip
  2. If you are not eligible to receive tax exempt benefits under the Indian Act, take the amount in box 14 and subtract any amount from box 18 (under "Other information" on your T4E slip)
  3. Enter the subtracted amount on line 11900 on your return

If your income is tax exempt

If your income is eligible for tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act, for details go to taxes and benefits for Indigenous peoples.

Other tax slips you may receive

You may also receive a T4A slip if you received COVID-19 benefit from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

Quebec residents will get both a T4E and a T4EQ slip.

If your employer received the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS) to cover part of your wages, the income you earned will appear on your regular T4 slip.
