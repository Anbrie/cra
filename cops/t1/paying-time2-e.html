Arrange to pay over time - Personal income tax

If you are unable to pay your amount owing in full, you have other options. You may be able to pay what you can afford as a partial payment or arrange to pay the amount you owe over a period of time.

On this page

  • Make a payment arrangement on your own
  • Call the CRA to make a payment arrangement
  • Adjust your payment arrangement

Make a payment arrangement on your own

If you cannot pay the full amount of your balance owing, but know you can afford to pay your balance over a period of time, you can set up a payment arrangement yourself without having to contact the CRA.

You can start paying what you owe by making a partial payment to the CRA. By doing this, you have entered a payment arrangement which will end once your balance is paid in full.

There are several options to set this up yourself, such as:

  • Pay by pre-authorized debit in CRA My Account
  • Pay through your financial institution
  • Send post-dated cheques to the CRA to cover your balance owing

For more options and other details: Payment options for individuals

Call the CRA to make a payment arrangement

If you have a more complex situation and need help to figure out how much you are able to pay, the CRA will work with you to find the best way to pay your amount owing.

You can make a payment arrangement with the CRA that allows you to make smaller payments to pay your amount owing over a period of time.

Steps to determine what you can afford to pay over time

  1. Calculate your monthly income and expenses

    Use the Personal income and expense worksheet to determine what you can afford to pay on a regular basis.

  2. Determine how much you can afford

    Use the Payment Arrangement Calculator to calculate:

    • your payment amount
    • the number of payments you want to make
    • how often you want to pay
    • estimated interest charged
    • when you want to finish paying your balance owing

  3. Set up your payment arrangement

    There are several ways you can set up a payment arrangement.

    You can also request an individual tax debt call.

  4. Start to make your payments

    Make your first payment to start your payment arrangement. You need to make a payment on all future agreed upon dates. Find options to make a payment.

    You must pay as agreed, continue to file all returns on time, and stay up to date with your tax obligations. Payment arrangements may be subject to periodic reviews.

    Even if you have a payment arrangement and are making payments, the CRA is authorized to take amounts from any benefits or credits you receive when you have a debt.

Adjust your payment arrangement

Should something happen and you need to adjust your payment arrangement or you are unable to pay, you must contact the CRA.

Failure to do this may cause the CRA to proceed with legal actions to collect the balance you owe.
